## 2. Overview

This website is a static informational offering for the open-source project JasyPDF. We use **no
tracking, no analytics tools and no third-party cookies**. Fonts and icons are **served locally from our
own server**, with **no connection to third-party servers** (in particular not to Google Fonts).
The only client-side storage is strictly necessary (see section 5).

## 3. Hosting

The legal basis is our legitimate interest in the secure and efficient provision of our online offering
pursuant to Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR.

## 4. Server log files

When you access the website, the host automatically collects and stores information in so-called server
log files, which your browser transmits automatically. These are typically:

- shortened or anonymized IP address
- date and time of access
- name and URL of the file accessed
- amount of data transferred
- browser and operating system used
- the previously visited page (referrer), if transmitted

This data is processed solely to ensure trouble-free operation and the security of the infrastructure
(Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR) and is not combined with other data sources. The log files are deleted after a
short period.

## 5. Local storage

We store strictly necessary settings exclusively locally in your browser (`localStorage`). This is not
tracking and not a third-party cookie. Specifically:

- your chosen appearance (light/dark mode)
- the note that you have already dismissed our privacy notice

This storage is strictly necessary for the function you requested; consent is not required for it
pursuant to § 25 (2) TDDDG. You can delete this data at any time via your browser settings.

## 6. Fonts and icons

The fonts used ("Inter", "Space Grotesk", "JetBrains Mono") and the icons are bundled locally at build
time and served from our own server. **No** data is transmitted to third parties (e.g. Google).

## 7. External links

This website contains links to external sites (e.g. to the source code on GitHub). When you click such
links, you leave our offering. We have no influence over the respective provider's data processing; that
provider's privacy policy applies there.

## 8. Your rights

Within the scope of the statutory provisions, you have the right at any time to:

- access to your processed data (Art. 15 GDPR)
- rectification of inaccurate data (Art. 16 GDPR)
- erasure (Art. 17 GDPR)
- restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
- data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
- object to the processing (Art. 21 GDPR)

An informal message to the email address above is sufficient to exercise these rights. You also have the
right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR).
